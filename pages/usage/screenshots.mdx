# Screenshots

Screenshots let you capture the current state of a browser session as a PNG image without leaving the session viewer. The image is saved to your session and can be downloaded at any time from Session History.

## Taking a Screenshot

1. Open an active session in the session viewer
2. Click the **Screenshot** button in the session toolbar
3. The capture happens instantly — a confirmation appears when the screenshot is saved

The screenshot captures the full visible browser viewport at the moment you click.

## Accessing Saved Screenshots

Screenshots are attached to the session they were taken in. To view or download them:

1. Go to [Session History](/usage/session-history)
2. Find the session and open its detail view
3. Screenshots are listed in the **Screenshots** tab — click any thumbnail to download

Screenshots are available for the lifetime of the session record. They are not deleted when the container stops.

## Limitations

| Constraint | Details |
|---|---|
| Format | PNG only |
| Scope | Captures the browser viewport — not the full page or off-screen content |
| Kali Linux | Screenshots work on Kali but capture the full desktop environment, not just the browser window |
| Storage | Screenshots are stored server-side; very large numbers of captures per session may affect performance |

## Why Take Screenshots?

Screenshots are useful for:

- Preserving evidence of what was seen during an investigation without downloading files
- Documenting threat actor infrastructure, phishing pages, or suspicious content
- Creating a lightweight audit trail alongside [network logs](/usage/network-logging)
