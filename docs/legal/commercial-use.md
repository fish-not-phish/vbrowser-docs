# Commercial Use

Open vBrowser (OvB) is released under a **custom non-commercial license**. This document clarifies what constitutes commercial use, what is permitted, and how to obtain authorization for commercial deployments.

This page is intentionally explicit to avoid ambiguity.

---

## License Summary

OvB is licensed for:

- Personal use
- Educational use
- Research use
- Internal evaluation

OvB is **not licensed for commercial use by default**.

---

## What Counts as Commercial Use

Commercial use includes, but is not limited to:

- Offering OvB as a paid service
- Reselling OvB access or subscriptions
- Embedding OvB into a commercial product
- Providing OvB access to clients as part of paid engagements
- Operating OvB within a revenue-generating business
- Charging for investigations performed using OvB

If money changes hands in connection with OvB, it is likely commercial use.

---

## What Is Allowed Without Permission

The following are permitted **without additional authorization**:

- Personal investigative use
- Academic or educational research
- Internal testing and evaluation
- Proof-of-concept deployments
- Non-profit research activities

These uses must still comply with applicable laws and policies.

---

## What Is NOT Allowed

The following are **explicitly prohibited without written permission**:

- Hosting OvB as a SaaS platform
- Managed service offerings built on OvB
- White-labeling or rebranding OvB
- Selling access to OvB instances
- Using OvB as part of a paid security service

Unauthorized commercial use violates the license.

---

## Internal Business Use

Use of OvB inside a for-profit organization **may constitute commercial use**, even if OvB is not sold directly.

Examples:
- Internal SOC tooling
- Client investigations
- Revenue-supporting security operations

When in doubt, assume permission is required.

---

## Requesting Commercial Permission

Commercial licensing may be available on a case-by-case basis.

To request permission:
- Describe your intended use
- Specify scale and deployment model
- Identify whether OvB will be customer-facing

Contact:
**jfisher@vbrowser.io**

---

## Enforcement

Unauthorized commercial use may result in:

- License revocation
- Termination of support
- Legal action

OvB is provided in good faith to the community.
Respecting the license ensures continued development.

---

## No Warranty

OvB is provided:

- "As is"
- Without warranty
- Without liability guarantees

Commercial users assume full responsibility for deployment and operation.

---

## Summary

If you are:
- Using OvB personally → Allowed
- Evaluating OvB internally → Allowed
- Providing OvB as a service → Permission required
- Using OvB in paid work → Permission required

When uncertain, ask before deploying.
